Objective:
The goal of Yatzy is to score the most points by rolling five dice to make specific combinations.
Setup:
Each player takes turns rolling the five dice.
A game consists of 13 rounds.
Players uses the scorecard on the right to choose what points they want.
Gameplay:
Roll Dice: On your turn, you can roll the dice up to three times.
After the first roll, you can choose to keep any dice and re-roll the others.
After the second roll, you can again choose to keep any dice and re-roll the others by pressing the hold button under the dice.
After the third roll, you must use the dice to score.
Scoring: After rolling, choose one of the 13 categories to score your dice. Each category can only be used once per game.
Upper Section:
Ones, Twos, Threes, Fours, Fives, Sixes: Sum of the dice showing the same face.
Lower Section:
Three of a Kind: Sum of all five dice if at least three are the same.
Four of a Kind: Sum of all five dice if at least four are the same.
Full House: 25 points for three of one number and two of another.

Small Straight: 30 points for a sequence of four (e.g., 1-2-3-4).
Large Straight: 40 points for a sequence of five (e.g., 1-2-3-4-5).
Chance: Sum of all five dice, any combination.
Yatzy: 50 points for five of a kind.
Bonus: If the total score in the upper section is 63 or more, you get a bonus of 35 points.
Max: This coloum show you the highest point you can get for every row.
End of Game:
After 13 rounds, the game ends.
The player with the highest total score wins.
Tips:
Strategically decide when to go for high-scoring combinations.
Balance between securing safe scores and attempting risky high-value rolls.
Enjoy playing Yatzy!
